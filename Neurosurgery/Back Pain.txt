Back Pain 
Which of the following is an accurate description of mechanical back pain?
A) Low back pain that is worse with prolonged standing and relieved by ambulation
B) Low back pain that is worse at rest and relieved by activity
C) Low back pain associated with fever and night sweats
D) Low back pain with saddle anaesthesia
Correct Answer: A) Low back pain that is worse with prolonged standing and relieved by ambulation
###
A 45-year-old worker presents with a 4-week history of lower back pain. The pain is
isolated to his lower back and he is not experiencing any signs of radiculopathy. What is
the best next step in management?
A) MRI without contrast
B) Conservative management (NSAIDs + physiotherapy)
C) Lumbar spine X-ray
D) Surgical referral
Correct Answer: C) Lumbar spine X-ray
###
On MRI of the lumbar spine, a disc herniation at L4/L5 is identified compressing a nerve
root. What is the most likely level affected?
A) L4 nerve root
B) L5 nerve root
C) S1 nerve root
D) L3 nerve root
Correct Answer: B) L5 nerve root
###
What are the most common symptoms of cervical myelopathy?
A) Bilateral hand weakness, numbness and pain (radiculopathy)
B) Bilateral hand clumsiness and gait instability
C) Unilateral leg weakness
D) Saddle anaesthesia
Correct Answer: B) Bilateral hand clumsiness and gait instability
###
A patient presents with loss of sensation below the umbilicus, paraplegia, and stool/urine
incontinence. What is the most likely level of injury?
A) T7 fracture
B) C7 fracture
C) Severe head trauma affecting motor area
D) Cauda equina
Correct Answer: A) T7 fracture
###
Which of the following tests does NOT evaluate spinal nerve root compression?
A) Straight leg raise test
B) FABER test
C) Kernig test
D) Lower extremities reflex testing
Correct Answer: B) FABER test
###
Which nerve root is affected by a C5/C6 disc herniation?
A) C5
B) C6
C) C7
D) C4
Correct Answer: B) C6
