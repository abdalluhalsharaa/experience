Subarachnoid Haemorrhage
What is the most sensitive test for detection of acute subarachnoid hemorrhage (SAH)?
A) Conventional 4-vessel cerebral angiogram
B) CTA brain
C) Lumbar puncture (LP)
D) CT brain without contrast
Correct Answer: C) Lumbar puncture (LP)
###
A 62-year-old female patient was admitted to the ICU with aneurysmal SAH. 6 days later,
she developed sudden aphasia and right-sided hemiplegia. What is the most likely cause?
A) Rebleeding from MCA aneurysm
B) Vasospasm in MCA
C) Vasospasm in ACA
D) Rebleeding from anterior communicating artery aneurysm
Correct Answer: B) Vasospasm in MCA
###
Which of the following is WRONG regarding intracranial aneurysms?
A) Peak age is 55-65 years
B) More common in males than in females
C) Rupture occurs in 95-97% of symptomatic cases
D) Berry aneurysms account for 75-80% of SAH
Correct Answer: B) More common in males than in females
###
A patient with SAH has GCS of 13-14 with no major deficit. What is the WFNS grade?
A. WFNS Grade 1
B. WFNS Grade 2
C. WFNS Grade 3
D. WFNS Grade 4
Correct Answer: B) WFNS Grade 2
###
All of the following are in favor of surgical clipping over coiling in the management of
intracranial aneurysms EXCEPT:
A) Young patient with long life expectancy
B) Aneurysm with wide neck
C) Hunt and Hess grade 4 on admission
D) Accessible aneurysm location
Correct Answer: C) Hunt and Hess grade 4 on admission
###
What is the treatment for vasospasm after SAH?
A) Triple H therapy (Hemodilution, Hypervolemia, Hypertension)
B) Mannitol
C) Dexamethasone
D) Aspirin
Correct Answer: A) Triple H therapy (Hemodilution, Hypervolemia, Hypertension)
